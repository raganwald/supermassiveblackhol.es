## First Draft

### Introduction

Good afternoon fellow students, staff, and families.

My name is Thomas Braithwaite. I have been a student at Wasdell for two years, and I am graduating from grade six.

Before coming to Wasdell, I was a student at Duke of Connaught in Toronto.

This is my Valedictorian Address: I am going to talk about how Wasdell changed me, and how Wasdell will change us for the rest of our life.

### Comparing Duke of Connaught to Wasdell

At Duke of Connaught, I had friends, but I also was bullied by other students.

At Duke of Connaught, the way they taught did not fit the way I learned.

So, I was not happy socially, and I was not learning.

At Wasdell, I am surrounded by good people.

At Wasdell, the way they teach fits the way I learn: We have small classes, and I get individual attention.


### How Wasdell Changed Me

I am more confident socially.

I was bullied, but now I now have good people around me.

I understand that I can pick good people to be in my life.

I am learning so much faster.

I was being taught with methods that did not fit me, but now I am now taught with methods that fit me.

I understand that I can learn, I just have to find the method that fits me properly.

### Our Life Ahead

At Wasdell, we are a small group of good people who get along and support each other.

We learn our own way.

We will be successful after Wasdell.

We will be around other good people and that we support and who will support us.

And we will keep learning.


### How We Will Succeed

Wasdell has taught us that there are good people that fit us.

Wasdell has taught us how to support our friends and to be supported.

Wasdell has taught us the importance of picking our friends.

We don't have to settle for whomever just happens to sit next to us.

We can choose good people, and choosing good people makes us--and them--succeed.

Wasdell has taught us that there are many ways to learn.

Wasdell has taught us that some ways to learn work really well for us.

Wasdell has taught us that if we are struggling, we can pick a better way to learn.

Wasdell has taught us that we can choose how to learn, and choosing good ways to learn makes us successful.

### Thank You

That was my Valedictorian Address.

I talked about how Wasdell taught me about good people and that there are better ways to learn.

I talked about how we will be successful after Wasdell by choosing good people and by choosing better ways to learn.

Thank you to the staff and students, and to the families that supported us.

Good luck everyone!
